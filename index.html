By S. Ghazarat

Introduction

Creating a project synopsis is a critical step for students, particularly in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the Islamic University of Science & Technology, Kashmir. This guide outlines the structure and formatting requirements for a B.Tech project synopsis, as specified in the university's guidelines, ensuring clarity and professionalism in your submission.

Formatting Guidelines

The synopsis must adhere to specific formatting standards to maintain consistency and readability:

  • Page Numbering: Use Roman numerals (i, ii, iii, ...) for Contents and Abstract; Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3, ...) from Introduction onwards, placed at the bottom center.
  • Printing: Use 1.5 line spacing, 12-point Times New Roman font, and double-sided printing.
  • Margins: Top: 3 cm, Bottom: 3 cm, Left: 4 cm, Right: 2 cm.
  • Paper Quality: Use good quality A4 paper.

Structure of the Synopsis

The synopsis should include the following components:

  • Cover Page: Follow Template 5, including project title, group number, student names with roll numbers, department, and submission date. No page number on the cover page.
  • Table of Contents: List all sections with page numbers.
  • List of Tables and Figures: Include all tables and figures with their respective titles and page numbers.
  • List of Symbols and Abbreviations: If applicable, provide a list of symbols and abbreviations used.
  • Abstract: A concise summary of the thesis content, highlighting new information and main conclusions, suitable for indexing or quoting.

Chapter Organization

The synopsis should be organized into the following chapters:

Chapter 1: Introduction

  • 1.1 Rationale: Explain the motivation behind the project.
  • 1.2 Problem Statement: Clearly describe the problem or challenge.
  • 1.3 Objectives: List specific, measurable outcomes to address the problem.
  • 1.4 Organization of Report: Outline the structure of the synopsis.

Chapter 2: Literature Survey

Discuss relevant prior work, with sub-headings developed in consultation with your supervisor.

Chapter 3: Methodology

  • 3.1 Gantt Chart: Provide a timeline of activities.
  • 3.2 Data Set Repository: Describe any datasets used, if applicable.
  • 3.3 Hardware and Software Requirements: Specify tools and technologies.
  • 3.4 Framework: Discuss the proposed framework or methodology.

Chapter 4: Conclusion and Future Scope

Summarize key findings and suggest potential improvements or extensions.

Numbering and Referencing

Proper numbering and referencing enhance clarity:

  • Equations: Numbered with chapter information, e.g., (2.1) for the first equation in Chapter 2.
  • Figures: Titles below figures, numbered chapter-wise (e.g., Fig. 2.1), with 300 dpi resolution. Reference in text before the figure.
  • Tables: Titles above tables, numbered chapter-wise (e.g., Table 2.1). Reference in text before the table.
  • References: Use IEEE style, listed in order of appearance (e.g., [1], [2]). Include at least 10 references after the Future Scope section.

Example IEEE references:

  • Journal: R.E. Higgs, K.G. Bemis, I.A. Watson, and J.H. Wikel, "Experimental designs for selecting molecules from large chemical databases," Journal of Chemical Information and Computer Sciences, vol. 37, no. 5, pp. 861-870, Sep. 1997.
  • Book: V.D. Liseikin, Scientific Computation: Grid Generation Methods, 2nd ed. New York: Springer Science, 2010.
  • Conference: N. Paivinen and T. Gronfors, "Minimum spanning tree clustering of EEG signals," in 6th Nordic Signal Processing Symposium (NORSIG-2004), Finland, Jun. 9-11, 2004, pp. 149-152.

Conclusion

Following these guidelines ensures a well-structured and professional B.Tech project synopsis. Adhering to the specified format, chapter organization, and referencing style will help convey your project's purpose and contributions effectively. Always consult with your supervisor for any minor adjustments to the format.
